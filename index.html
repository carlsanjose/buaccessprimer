About Us

Our Mission:

The BU Academic Consortium of Computer Science Students strives to reinforce the structure of today's future thinkers to endow the capacity of tomorrow's envisioned innovators for a revolutionized future.

Our Vision:

An organization that upholds the welfare of its constituents through significant and sustainable experiences that cultivate efficient, reliable, goal-oriented, and future-ready individuals.

Our Objectives:

  1. Strive to promote, improve, and develop among students increased knowledge and greater interest and understanding in computer and information technology.
  2. Encourage and promote continuing research and study, and may adopt other measures as necessary, to foster education and advancement in the field of computer technology.
  3. Serve as a vessel for computer technology awareness and consciousness through intellectual inquiry and information exchange among its members; and
  4. Strive to meet a high degree of academic standards and technical excellence in the field of computer technology through its members.

Boot Up CS: KumsayEssentials.lib

Event Description

This event is designed specifically for freshmen Computer Science students to help them better understand and navigate the fundamentals of C programming. It aims to provide them with essential tools, tips, and tricks that will serve as a strong foundation for their journey into coding. To achieve this, two speakers will be invited: one upper-year student who will share practical insights on C programming, and an alumnus who will impart wisdom and technical advice gained from their own academic and professional experiences. By blending peer guidance and alumni mentorship, the session will give participants a clearer perspective on coding, foster confidence in tackling programming challenges, and inspire them to maximize their learning potential.

Objectives

The main goal of KumsayEssenitials.lib is to support freshmen BS Computer Science students in building their programming foundation while nurturing a supportive learning community. Specifically, it aims to:

  • Introduce the fundamentals of C programming and its role in the CS curriculum;
  • Equip students with practical tips, tools, and strategies for effective coding;
  • Encourage peer support by fostering connections among freshmen and higher-year students;
  • Highlight student speakership by providing a platform for upper-year students to share their knowledge and experiences.
